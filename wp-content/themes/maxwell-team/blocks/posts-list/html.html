Link Building Strategies That Actually Work in 2024

Forget outdated tactics. Here are the link building strategies that are generating real results for B2B tech companies.

Lisa Thompson

December 20, 2023

9 min read

Link building remains one of the most important—and most challenging—aspects of SEO. But the tactics that worked five years ago can now get you penalized. Here's what's actually working for B2B tech companies in 2024.

The State of Link Building

Google's algorithms have become remarkably sophisticated at detecting manipulative link building. The focus has shifted from quantity to quality, and from manipulation to earning links through genuine value.

Strategies That Work

1. Original Research and Data

Nothing attracts links like being the original source of interesting data. Conduct surveys, analyze your product data (anonymized), or compile industry benchmarks. When journalists and bloggers need to cite statistics, they'll link to you.

2. Digital PR

Build relationships with journalists and publications in your industry. Offer expert commentary on trending topics, provide data for stories, and position your executives as thought leaders.

3. Resource Link Building

Create genuinely useful resources—tools, calculators, templates, comprehensive guides—that people naturally want to reference and link to.

4. Strategic Partnerships

Partner with complementary (non-competing) companies for co-marketing initiatives. Guest posts, joint webinars, and collaborative research all generate natural backlinks.

Tactics to Avoid

  • Paid links (Google explicitly penalizes this)
  • Low-quality guest posting at scale
  • Link exchanges and link schemes
  • Private Blog Networks (PBNs)
  • Automated outreach with generic templates

The Outreach Process

  • Effective outreach is personal and value-focused:
  • Research the target site and author thoroughly
  • Personalize every email—reference their recent work
  • Lead with value—what's in it for them?
  • Follow up respectfully (2-3 times maximum)
  • Track and measure response rates

Measuring Link Building Success

  • Look beyond just the number of links:
  • Domain authority/rating of linking sites
  • Relevance of linking sites to your industry
  • Traffic from referral links
  • Anchor text distribution
  • Link velocity (natural growth over time)

Building a Sustainable Program

  • The best link building programs are sustainable because they focus on creating genuine value. Invest in:
  • A dedicated content team producing link-worthy assets
  • PR and relationship-building capabilities
  • Tools for prospecting and outreach management
  • Ongoing measurement and optimization

Quick Wins

  • While building your long-term strategy, here are some quick wins:
  • Reclaim unlinked brand mentions
  • Fix broken links pointing to your site
  • Update and republish old high-performing content
  • Leverage existing partnerships for link opportunities
